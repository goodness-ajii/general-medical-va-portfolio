## Purpose

This sample shows how I plan and run a multi-day conference trip for a founder. The goal is not to hand the CEO a calendar — it is to give them a frictionless trip where every transition is anticipated, every meeting has prep, and nothing requires them to think about logistics.

## 8. Daily Prep Packet — What Lands in the CEO's Inbox by 5 PM the Night Before

A clean one-page brief for each day:

- Tomorrow's full schedule with locations and walking times
- One-pager per meeting:
   - Who the person is and the org
   - Most recent public news about them
   - Helix's prior interactions
   - Three suggested talking points
   - One thing to avoid bringing up (if relevant)
- Notes on any meeting where the CEO needs to make a decision or commitment
- Reminders (water, meds, charge phone, business cards count)
- A single line: "Anything you want me to handle differently? Reply or Slack."

## 12. Post-Trip Deliverables (By Monday, June 15)

I deliver these without being asked:

- Trip recap memo: goals vs. actuals, KPI scorecard, reflections
- Meeting notes from every investor and partner conversation, in standard format (see Sample 01)
- Follow-up tracker: every commitment made, owner, due date, status
- Customer development synthesis: themes across the 4 founder conversations
- Pipeline update: every investor moved from cold to warm, warm to active
- Expense report submitted to finance
- Thank-you notes drafted for every meeting (CEO reviews and signs)
- Trip retrospective: what worked, what to change for next conference

## 13. Why This Document Matters in Hiring

When a founder reads this kind of plan, they realize:

- I think about **transitions, not just appointments** — every gap is anticipated
- I separate **logistics from content** — the COO owns content, I own logistics
- I deliver **daily prep packets** so the CEO is never walking in cold
- I track **post-trip follow-through** so the value of the trip is captured, not lost
- I respect **the CEO's energy** — rest blocks, breakfast space, evening recovery are deliberate
- I plan for **the unexpected** — emergency contacts, urgent care, backups
