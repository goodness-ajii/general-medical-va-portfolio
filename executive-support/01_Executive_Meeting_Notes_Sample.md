## 5. Discussion Summary

### 5.1 Cordova's thesis (Elena Vargas)
Cordova is actively deploying out of Fund III. Their MedTech thesis centers on regulatory intelligence, post-market surveillance, and AI-driven quality systems. They typically lead Series A rounds at $8M–$15M check size, with a preference for companies that can show ARR growth tied to a wedge product before broadening the platform. Recent comparable investments referenced: two unnamed portfolio companies in clinical-trials infrastructure and post-market analytics.

### 5.2 Helix overview and traction (Dr. Reed)
Dr. Reed walked through Helix's core platform, the regulatory intelligence engine launched after the seed, and the wedge: medical device innovators preparing for FDA submissions. Traction shared: pipeline of design partners, two paid pilots converted, and revenue trajectory through Q2. Roadmap framed around three layers — design control, regulatory intelligence, and compliance scoring — with Series A use of funds focused on the first two.

### 5.3 Q&A (Cordova)
- **On wedge vs. platform.** Daniel Okafor asked how Helix prioritizes between the wedge product and the broader platform vision. Dr. Reed answered that the wedge is the revenue engine for the next 18 months and the platform layers compound on top.
- **On regulatory moat.** Elena Vargas asked how Helix protects against FDA guidance changes invalidating model outputs. Dr. Reed walked through the human-in-the-loop validation model and the regulatory advisory board.
- **On go-to-market.** Daniel asked about CAC and sales motion. Maya Chen answered with current channel mix and the founder-led GTM motion through Series A.
- **On team.** Elena asked about hiring plans through Series A. Dr. Reed flagged the VP Product and Head of Regulatory Affairs hires as priorities.

### 5.4 Next steps (joint)
Cordova requested a formal pitch with Elena's full partnership in June. They asked for a data room link and the most recent investor update. Helix agreed to send both within five business days.
